# The Riddle of Steel IX

"Horang... why Horang!? Why her...?"

**Bunsen** weeps as he cradles the body of his fallen granddaughter, last of his line. **Ganent** attempts to comfort him, but has no promises of eternal afterlife to offer. He muses on the impression he felt at dawn. A feeling had come to him, an impression of the sound Aglacia worked into his head. Though he has renounced his old clerical life, something deep within him stirs.

The Book pick over their dead. Their defacto leader, The Burn, has fallen. **The Fiend** calls to remember The Burns wish for absolute magical power, but the others are not convinced. While **Bunsen** and **Ganent** build a pyre to cremate their fallen, The Book investigate the huts of the old hags. Inside they find poison (which **The Fiend** drinks), a cure-all (which **The Fiend** uses to cure his poisoning), a letter written in an unknown language, several bags of treasure, and a banner bearing the symbol of an eye with fangs (previously seen amongst the snake men!)

In one strangely (magically) opulent cabin, a naked mantied to a bed. He yells for help and, after being untied, introduces himself as **Simon**, a sailor aboard the Everblizzard, part of the same expedition as The Books ship. He washed ashore and had been surviving in the wilderness alone. When the fishmen attacked, he spotted their fire on the beach and approached, but he fell unconscious. When next he awoke he was here.

The Book continue to explore areas previously skipped. They find a secret passage leading to a grotto. A staircase leads up to the back side of the tower sitting atop The Sword. The old door is quickly broken through. Within is a very old cracked fresnel lens. The Book discuss bringing it back with them, but it's too heavy. Upon a table they find another letter, written in the same script as the previous letter, but with worse handwriting.

Back through the Grotto they find a courtyard overgrown with coral. Strange zombie-like men are affixed to the wall, merged with the sea plants. It is determined they are undergoing some sort of metamorphoses. A strange pool in the middle of the yard bewitches **The Fiend**, who almost sinks down into the pool. He is rescued by **The Gelid**. The pool then flickers into a moving image! From the point of view of a fishman, **The Fiend** and **The Gelid** watch a raid on a small ramshackle village perched on a beach. Loribellian shouts can be heard, but this settlement is not the cave. Other survivors! The vision ends when the fish man is killed, their raid repelled.

The Burn is cremated inside one of the hags huts while Horang is put to rest on the pyre. **Bunsen** hopes that her soul will be free to return to Loribel. **Ganent** offers prayers to the loribelian gods Lecia and Exaldios, but he cannot feel even the smallest weight of their presence or attention. Privately, he offers a prayer to this new feeling that has visited him with the dawn. Crow is wrapped in a sheet and given to the ocean, flying through the air one last time.

The Book descends The Sword, stopping to rescue some treasure from the harpy lair ship (a +1 cutlass, some mariners armor and some additional treasure will come in handy!). At the base of the cliff they are relieve to find the rest of the volunteers safe.

Before they can leave, the wish to check out the fish mens lair.
