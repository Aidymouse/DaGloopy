# The Riddle of Steel XVI

That night, The Book have an intense discussion. **Shin** presents unknown dangers to the books goals as well as the admiral. **Ganent** is adamant to broker peace between the two camps, while the others, mostly **Simon** want to try and distance themselves from possible conflict. **The Fiend** is emphatically in favour of following this new sorcerous lord.

The Book venture to the camp the next day. The village is situated on a hill. Stone ruins dot the hill, capped by a great spire at its tip.  They are greeted at the gate with a warm reception. The citizens of the settlement, named New Loribel, are excited to meet the settlers of the old camp. **Shin** sleeps in the spire during the day and the book are encouraged not to wake him for fear of his enourmous temper. The Book spend the daytime exploring the settlement.

**Simon** visits the forge and acquires some bolts for his crossbow. **Ganent** visits the church helmed by **Father Lemmin**. Though the father has been having more visions than usual since coming to the new land, he doesn't pay any heed to the idea of a new religion, nor to the carving that **Ganent** finds in the floor, which seems to depict many worm-like creatures similar to the dragon he saw before. **The Gelid** investigates a parthenon-like structure in the center of the village. There he meets **Big Gob**. He also finds a map of the surrounding wilderness with several points marked.

The book are invited for dinner where they will meet **Shin**. **Ganent** tries to learn rumors of his reputation while gathering oil from a dead whale nearby. The crew seem to trust in the power of their captain, though he can be a bit like a loose cannon.

The Book settle down for dinner and have a thunderous introduction to **Shin** as he smashes down the door to his tower. The captain is a hulking brute of a man, at least 9 feet tall. He carries a slab of stone in the rough shape of a sword, wielding the impossible weight with ease. He is followed by **Chochu**, a lithe long-haird dark-skinned character who himself stands at least 7 feet tall, his limbs unnaturally long. The pair wear little clothing between them, **Shin** being clad in a leopards pelt fashinoned into a kilt and **Chochu** being draped in silk.

As guests of honor, The Book are seated next to the captain, at the head of a long feast table in the parthenon. In contrast to their speculation, **Shin** turns out to be a brute with rocks where his brain should be. His sorcerous knowledge is replaced with an assumption of god-given power derived from a magical ring adorning his finger. It is little mystery where **Shins** dissenters are. **The Fiend**, enthralled by the show of strength, declares his loyalty almost immediately. **Shin** is well pleased with this, but almost flattens his new "prince" after **The Fiend** lets loose his dancing lights. It seems he does not know much about magic at all. **Ganent** attempts to reconcile the situation by stating that their abilities can be an aid to **Shin**, which he takes to mean can be used to enhance his rightful strength.

**Chochu** does not introduce himself, nor speak to anyone at the table. He does not speak at all. **The Warden** attempts to use a minor illusion to communicate with him but is spotted by **Shin** and told off with a roar. The captain is very protective of his lieutenant.

The Book quickly find themselves walking on eggshells attempting to gain information without giving too much away. **Shin** has dreams of empire, in a childish kind of way. Statescraft does not seem to be in his lexicon. Still, he says his goal is to be peace in the region (by way of assimilating all others by force). The fact of **Shin** being more a violent force of nature than a competent sorcerer complicates the plans of The Book, who start to find themselves the only vanguard between warring barbarians and the pride of the admiral.
