# The Riddle of Steel XV

**Ganent** tends to the half-transformed rescued villagers. Among them, **Gemerald** explains, is **Corin**, a member of the 1st expedition! Their brief encounter with snake magic has left them delirious, but healthy enough.

Before they rest, The Book investigate a sunken tower in their vicinity. **The Enmity** squeezes through the small entrance, but his spell goes awry when he conjures a light, and he shrinks even further! The transformation is short-lived, but lasts long enough to affect their encounter with a giant mass of ochre-ooze! **The Fiend** suffers heavy wounds, but **The Gelid** manages to pop the ooze before it can deal a killing blow. In the tower, The Book find an orb that glows with a moon when it is night and the sun when it is day. The entire group rests in the tower that night.

The next morning, the prisoners have regained composure. The Book grill **Corin** for details about his first expedition. He explains that his camp, nestled in an old ruin in a mountain, was led by Admiral Flint for around 6 months, but he was killed in a coup by the magic-user **Shin**. In private, The Book discuss their best prospects given this unexpected development. **Shin**, who seems to have similar ideas to their own, may not cooperate with them, and the last thing they need is to throw their lot in with a usurper and get caught between two enemies. In the end, they decide to send the villagers back to camp with **Bunsen** while they head north to find the second expedition.

The Book travel up the coast for a few days. They pass a rocky cove with a crashed ship, but decide to come back later. One day, as the sun is setting, **The Crouton** spots flying shapes through his telescope. Drawing their attention, The Book witness a battle between two gargantuan flying snake-creatures, lit by the setting sun. In one crucial instant, one of the snakes lunges for the other, scoring a lethal hit in its throat. The beast falls beneath the shadowy crest of sunset. A pit forms in **Ganent**s stomach.

The next morning, **Ganent** announces to the group his request to go investigate the fallen snake, fearing he would be a poor acolyte of his new god (whom he suspects is one of these snake creatures) if he did not try to find the body of one that had fallen. To his surprise, they agree. **Simon** spends the day hunting on the shore while **Ganent**, **The Gelid**, **The Enmity** and **The Fiend** search for the body.

Only a few hours later, their boat intersects with the trail end of the great flowing tail. The beast is impossible larger than they've imagined. Already it is being picked apart by fish. **Ganent** sheds his armor and sword and dives into the ocean, clambering onto the beasts scales. In one of its wounds he finds a long serrated tooth, warm to the touch no matter how long he holds it. He also finds some white stringy hair. Eventually he comes to the beasts head. It does not look like a snake, but more resembles a dragon from Loribelian folklore (though those are four-legged). He utters a soft prayer for the life lost. On the way back to the shore, **Ganent** and **The Enmity** wax philosophical about the nature of gods, mortals and death.

The expedition continues up the coast for few more days, guided by **The Croutons** telescope and **Ganents** recollection of his vision. When the mountains can be seen over the horizon, The Book encounter a coral reef and must beach the ship. The hide it among the trees, then begin the long trek into the hills.


But their trek doesn't isn't long. When the first hill is crested, they get a look into a deep mountain valley. There, nestled among the rock, is their first look at the ruins inhabited by the first expedition. **Shin**, whether he is their enemy or ally, waits there.

---

**Ganent** spends his nights in consideration and prayer. Each morning, with the rising sun, he tries to recall that feeling that first visited him atop hag rock. He is weary of getting his hopes up, for his past is shrouded in black clouds because of this very folly, but in the calm breeze as the empty continent wakes up, he feels that maybe contentment could be possible in this place.

Ganent lies awake, tucked into a corner of the small, crowded boat. The light from the crescent moon is faint, illuminating the shapes of strangers he has begun to trust huddled under blankets of their own. He shivers and slouches down deeper into the hull. The wind is cold on the open water. His eyes are fixed upon the stars, but his mind runs over the image of the colossus that lay slain on the surface. The wounds it bore, the scale of its maw, the tooth...

He presses his hand to his tunic where it remains tucked, wrapped in the tress of silvery hair that remained on the beast. It is still as warm as when he drew it from the Red Wyrm's wound, bringing some comfort in a land where he has found little. He remains, in thought, for hours. The moon arcs towards the horizon and his gaze follows it west as the watch changes. Something wills him to rest, to recover his strength, and though his mind continues to work for answers his body complies.

In sleep Ganent finds communion once again. He skims above the ocean's surface at unimaginable speeds, shadow silent, headed toward the first lip of a rising sun. His mind is quiet, but the emotion he feels outweighs need for language. Respect. Gratitude. Certainty. Hope. Feelings wash over his conscience like the rising sun's heat rippling over sky blue scales. 

It is all too soon that he finds himself awakened by the cold touch of the Gelid. He blinks, nods, and takes his post silently, watching the sky grow red for the second time as dawn silhouettes the mountains to the east.
