# The Riddle of Steel XIII

The Book head north rather than explore the cave in which they've slept. Finding their way blocked by the river, they venture deeper into the north-western forest. Here the terrain becomes much tougher. **The Fiend** is corrupted once again when a bonfire spell goes awry. His skin transmogrifies into paper! As nightfall approaches, The Book find themselves walking further and further into a static mist. **Ganent** accidentally invokes a magical detection spell when invoking a prayer to his deity, and spots a teleporting shape. He persues, leaving his companions, only to step right through a human skull! At that moment, the creature in the fog attacks - a spectral tiger!

Tracking the beast as it moves through the fog is difficult, but The Book eventually put the beast down. Thinking no local creature will bother them in the lair of the preadtor, they set down to rest.

During the night, **Simon** and **The Fiend** share plans to replace the admiral. Unbeknownst to them, **Ganent** is awake and listening! 

When **Ganent** takes watch early in the morning he hears the distant slithery chatting of snake men. The Book consider pursuing, but realise they are low on provisions and have little hope of crossing the river, especially since **The Fiend** is now made of paper. They decide to turn back.

On their way past the cave in which they fought the brain-rats, The Book decide to explore. They find a waterfall aside a cliff (which **Ganent** fails to climb, injuring himself) and vow to come back at some point. They make it down to the monastery and take a rest.

Due to his injuries and fatigue, **Ganent** dozes off during his watch. At that very moment, a giant snake slithers into camp. In a half-awake daze, **Ganent** watches the snake transform into a human with snake arms. He is rudely awakened when it leaps at him and attacks! **Ganent** is badly wounded immediately, but **The Gelids** jade flute scares the snake-man into fleeing. **The Crouton** prevents the escape attempt with a massive earthern hand, and The Book put the snake man down. They find another letter on his corpse. **Simon** spots weakness in **Ganent**.

The following day, The Book arrive at The Aerie. However, they get to the treacherous staircase as the sun is setting. With no other way around, they decide to risk the stairs. Before they do so, **The Crouton** casts a feather fall spell into **The Gelids** magic spell-storing stone recovered from the hydra. It's a good thing he does, too, as The Book make it only half the way down before **The Crouton** slips and falls, taking the whole book down with him. The spell in the pearl arrests the fall and The Book float harmlessly down onto the sand, safely back home.
