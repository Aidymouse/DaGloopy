# The Riddle of Steel VII

The Book of White Names consider their next move.

The best move seems to be moving the survivors further back into the cave, but with so many injured it's unsure how long that would take. Even then, it's not a guarantee that they'll be protected against the fish men.

**The Crow** visits **Bunsen** and his crew (Bunsen's Burners), reviewing some of their wounds. **The Burn** tags along, partly to keep tabs on his enemy, partly to gloat about their successful retrieval of the healing supplies.

To their surprise, **Bunsen** has a plan. Before the attack, he heard a great howl echo from the great sword of rock out on the coast. He surmises that the fish men are being led by an single coordinate (dubbed The Ocean Master). **Bunsens** plan is thus: a coordinated spearhead attack to the sword of rock. He has several warriors on board already. The Book of White Names mull over this idea. On one hand, it seems risky and hasty. There's no guarantee the ocean master resides on the rock, nor that destroying the ocean master will protect the survivors. On the other hand, The Book are not overly concerned with the lives of the survivors and the lure of a great magical power is tantalising. Their single greatest lead toward magical power points toward the sword. They agree to come with. **The Burn** also remarks that a treacherous attack on an enemy base is the perfect chance to eliminate Bunsen and his crew, if the need should arise.

While The Book waits for the rafts to be built, **The Crow** takes **Bunsens** brother *gulp* into the cave to perform a healing ritual (cure wounds). The members of The Book also level up!

When the rafts are complete, it is evening. After darkness falls, they set out toward the sword. They have some difficulty navigating the fog that has settled over the lake-still ocean. Despite the threat of sharks, they find their landing spot.

The landing holds a collection of metal cages, each housing a skeleton of a long dead sailor. In the center stands a barnacle-covered altar, stained with fresh blood. Worryingly, a kelp torch sits stuck to the wall, still lit. Someone is around.

The Book split off and explore up the cliff, clambering up a steep wet staircase. They find a harpies lair nestled in a crashed ship over a cliff. **The Enmity** narrowly avoids an ambush from the Matriarch Harpy, and is rescued by **The Wardens** hypnotic gaze which plummets the matriarch into the fog below. She does not return.

Still there are other harpies to deal with. Bunsen's Burners are lured by the sounds of combat - and of spells. In the aftermath of the fight, **Bunsen** finally confronts the book about his suspicion of their magical abilities. The Book see no point in hiding it now, and after a tense moment, **Bunsen** reveals his own proclivity for magic. His aims in these new lands are to build a nation where magic is accepted rather than feared. The Book and The Burners may prove to be useful allies to one another.

At least, for now.
