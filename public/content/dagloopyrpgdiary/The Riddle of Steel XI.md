# The Riddle of Steel XI

The Book have changed course, heading north west after **Ganent** realizes he's been recalling the mountain in his vision in the wrong direction. **The Gelid** attempts to conjure a compass, but it proves too much for him, and he loses control of this spell. To the horror of The Book, all of their heads growing spikes. This does not bode well for their return to the admiral.

After only half a days travel they have stumbled across a cave. The yawning pit stretches 40' across and 100' downwards. A strange powder lining the bottom of the pit catches the sun, sending it back in a scattered array of metallic orange and blue. **The Warden** is brave enough to descend the pit on a rope, but his magical detection spell reveals nothing. He does, however, spy a hole leading further down. An ancient barricade is easily circumvented and The Book travel down into the cave.

The first chamber is a scene of old warfare. Dry bones litter the cave, mostly human, though a few have snake-like torsos. As **Simon's** bullseye lantern sweeps across the dark, a skittering is heard down one of the exits. **The Enmity**, ever the friend of stone, discerns that the cave is an ancient mine, and far pre-dates any known inhabitants of the island. Ancient rails move down one corridor, but The Book follow the skittering.

The find it's source - kobolds! A quick battle ensues around a smoky fire. The Book are victorious. Exploring their surroundings, they find a cliff that drops into a pool of water. **The Enmity** drops a light-imbued rock into the pool and a dark shape is seen swimming. They also find a staircase leading down, which they take.

The book comes to a room full of barrels. Something moves within a barrel as it is pried open, but there is nothing but rocks inside! The rocks are ovoid in shape. The reflection of their torchlight catches and scatteres blue and orange fleks. Evidently the source of the powder above. As they're investigating, a raucous shriek of indignation fills the cave. It is **Nilbog**, guardian of the stones, which it calls its food. Indeed, nibbles are seen taken out of the stone. The Books quick negotiation strikes an alliance with the magical spirit. In exchange for clearing out more kobolds, **Nilbog** will tell them more about the cave.

The Book return to the battle room and follow the minecart tracks. The tracks themselves terminate at another cliff, this time dropping far below into a wide river of rushing water. A side passage leads them to the base of a small cliff upon which stands a kobold brimming with fire. He points a gnarled finger at the book and a fire snake emerges! **Ganent** focuses his new magic power and abjures the snake, which flees. While it is gone, **The Crouton** bends space and teleports the kobold mage down to the level of the party. His head is erupted by **Simons** crossbow. When the snake returns, The Book has prepared all manner of spells, and the beast is extinguished.

Atop the cliff, the mage was hoarding various metal devices the meaning of which cannot be ascertained by the book. Their construction is alien. Two gems are retrieved. They look to be the same stones as hoarded by **Nilbog**, but cut into clear gems. Fleks of glowing embers are seen swirling within. **The Crouton** finds a knife that looks to be made of the same material. There is also an iron door which cannot be opened.

The Book return to **Nilbog**, victorious. The sprite is overjoyed now that it has the run of the place. The Book want its help fighting the dark shape in the water, and eventually cajole it into helping by pointing out that it cannot truly be lord of the cave while monsters still lair within it.

Many hundreds of feet down a staircase the book emerge on the edge of the river. The bank is covered in machines much like were held by the kobold wizard, but much larger in size. **Nilbog** leads the party to the pool that is fed by the river, and beckons the creature to emerge. The surface of the water ripples and a great scaly head emerges. It is larger than The Book had reckoned. And it is followed by four more - a hydra!

Suddenly **Nilbogs** taunts don't seem like such a good idea.
